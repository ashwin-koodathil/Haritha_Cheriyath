About Me

I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Mathematical Modeling (CMM), Universidad de Chile, working with Sebastián Donoso. My research lies at the intersection of dynamical systems theory, with a particular focus on understanding the complex behaviors that emerge in mathematical systems over time.

Prior to my current position, I was a visiting fellow at the prestigious Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR) in Mumbai, where I continued to develop my expertise in advanced mathematical research. I completed my PhD at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Bhopal under the guidance of Dr. Nikita Agarwal.

Beyond my research, I am actively involved in the academic community. I co-organize the CMM postdoctoral seminar series SIPo with Georgios Kontogeorgiou, fostering collaboration and knowledge exchange among early-career researchers in mathematics. I have been awarded the prestigious Fondecyt postdoctoral program (2025) and NBHM postdoctoral fellowship (2023).

Research Interests

Ergodic Theory

Investigating the long-term behavior of dynamical systems and their statistical properties, with applications to understanding chaos and regularity in mathematical models.

Symbolic Dynamics

Exploring discrete dynamical systems through symbolic representations, bridging the gap between abstract mathematical theory and computational applications.

Open Dynamics

Studying systems with holes and escapes, understanding how openings in phase space affect the dynamics and statistical properties of mathematical systems.

Topological Dynamics

Examining the topological aspects of dynamical systems, focusing on the geometric and structural properties that govern system behavior.
